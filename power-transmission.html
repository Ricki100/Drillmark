Sprockets

Chain sprockets connect the shafts to the chains. They must, therefore, not only ensure a reliable connection between the shaft and nub in shafts of different geometry, but their tooth profile must also fit the chain used as well as possible. This is the only way to ensure reliable, low-friction, and low-noise operation. This allows chains of different designs (e.g. roller, sleeve, or toothed chains) and pitch (e.g. 6.35 mm, 8 mm, 9.525 mm) to be used, as well as simplex and duplex chains. When wheel material and manufacturing processes are selected, the load to which the chain sprocket will be subjected, the geometry to be implemented, and operating efficiency are all taken into account. A basic distinction is made between the following types:

  • Fine blanked sprockets
  • Sprockets made from sintered metal
  • Machined sprockets
  • Forged/extruded sprockets
Pulleys

At Drillmark, we stock a range of Power Transmission pulleys that are:

  • Manufactured according to relevant ISO or RMA standards to help ensure optimum performance
  • Statically-balanced to G6.3 balancing quality
  • Equipped with taper bushes for quick and accurate installation
  • Phosphate-coated for corrosion resistance

Chain Pulleys are attached to the hydraulically actuated upper section of the mast and serve to deflect the pulling chain.

Due to their relatively thick-walled outer ring, the Chain Pulley can accommodate high radial loads. These loads are resulting from the deadweights of the fork support, the forks and the payload. The outer ring profile is dictated by the dimensions and design of the pulling chain used. The two lips on the outer ring profile are providing lateral guidance of the chain. The distance between the two ball rows along with their contact angle care for a wide load support base, so that the Chain Pulleys can also absorb tilting and axial guiding forces.

Conveyor Chains

Effective lubrication is essential

Depending on the product they move, chain conveyors can face the same tough operating conditions that affect roller tables. Coil conveyors, for example, operate with high loads and temperatures. Consequently, effective lubrication of the chain is critical for trouble-free operation and longer conveyor service life. Our chain solutions can help ensure proper chain lubrication in the toughest operating conditions.

Roller Chains

A well-designed power transmission chain drive transmits mechanical power from one shaft to another efficiently. Transmission chains we supply are manufactured in state of the art facilities, using tight quality controls. All products conform to ISO standards and other industrial standards such as ANSI, BS, DIN and JIS.

While conforming to the standard ANSI for sprocket fitment, the H, SH, SPH and SHH series of transmission chains offer excellent strength and load capacity for demanding applications, and shock loading.
